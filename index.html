Chapter One

The Birth of a Pirate

In the lawless seas of the 18th century Caribbean, a Welsh sailor's ambition would reshape the world — and test the very souls of two ancient brotherhoods.

1715 — The Caribbean

A Sailor Becomes a Legend

Edward Kenway was not born a pirate. He was a Welsh privateer — brash, reckless, and burning with ambition — who dreamed of returning home to Bristol draped in gold and glory, enough to win the heart of his beloved Caroline.

When his ship is wrecked during a fierce storm, Edward survives against impossible odds, washing ashore on a desperate stretch of ocean. It is here that fate — and a fleeing Assassin named Duncan Walpole — changes everything.

"I spent years searching the seas for something I believed lay just over the horizon — treasure, glory, freedom. What I found instead was far more terrible, and far more precious."
— Edward Kenway

Chapter Two

Stolen Robes, Stolen Destiny

Killing the Assassin Duncan Walpole, Edward steals his robes and assumes his identity — posing as a member of the Brotherhood to sell their secrets to the Templars. But greed has a way of weaving webs more tangled than any man can escape.

What begins as a scheme for profit thrusts Edward into the heart of a centuries-old war. The Caribbean's most notorious pirates — Blackbeard, Charles Vane, Anne Bonny, Mary Read — become his allies, enemies, and mirrors of his own soul.

"Stealing robes does not make you an Assassin, Edward. But perhaps... wearing them long enough will."
— Adéwalé, Edward's Quartermaster

Chapter Three

The Observatory & The Sage

The Templars seek a mythical structure of immense power — the Observatory — a First Civilization installation capable of watching any person on Earth through a single drop of blood. To find it, they need Bartholomew Roberts, the mysterious Sage.

Edward's pursuit of the Observatory will take him from Nassau's sun-bleached taverns to Mayan ruins hidden beneath the jungle canopy — and force him to confront whether freedom was ever what he truly wanted.

The Ship

The Jackdaw

More than a vessel — she is Edward's freedom made manifest, his greatest treasure and fiercest weapon upon the Caribbean waves.

Man-O'-War Class Brig

Born from the Waves

The Jackdaw began her life as a modest brig, captured from the wreckage of Edward's first naval battle. Over years of raiding merchant ships and Royal Navy warships, she was upgraded into one of the most formidable vessels in the Caribbean.

Her black sails strike terror. Her broadside cannons can level a Man-o'-War. And at her helm stands Edward Kenway — singing sea shanties as enemy ships burn on the horizon.

The Chronicle

A Timeline of Blood & Salt

From the shores of Wales to the ruins of the First Civilization — Edward Kenway's journey spans a decade of war, legend, and loss.

1693
Edward Kenway Born
Born in Swansea, Wales, the son of a farmer. From childhood, Edward dreams of wealth and adventure far beyond the fields.
1712
Marriage to Caroline Scott
Edward weds Caroline against her father's wishes. Desperate to prove himself, he sails as a privateer — and never truly comes home.
1715
The Walpole Deception
After killing the fleeing Assassin Duncan Walpole, Edward steals his identity and robes — selling Brotherhood secrets to the Templars in Havana for gold.
1716
Captain of the Jackdaw
Edward captures a brig and christens her the Jackdaw. With Adéwalé as quartermaster, he begins his reign as one of the Caribbean's most feared pirates.
1718
The Fall of Nassau
Britain offers pardons to pirates. The Republic of Pirates fractures. Blackbeard dies at Ocracoke Inlet. The Golden Age of Piracy begins its twilight.
1722
Initiation into the Brotherhood
Having witnessed the cost of greed — in lives, in love, in friends lost — Edward Kenway is finally, truly, initiated as a Master Assassin of the Caribbean Brotherhood.
